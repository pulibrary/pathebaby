Abstract: 
Henry IV, 1st chapter, Siege of Paris, No. 6. Henry IV decided to go survey the edges of the capital. Ruggieri, provided with the letter stolen from the injured messenger, disguised himself as a pilgrim returning from the Holy Land, hastening towards Paris ... Don't you see, over there, behind the trees, ill-looking soldiers? I am going to teach you, scoundrels, to mistreat women! It's HIM! Would you let us continue our journey . . . we are the daughters of a Spanish gentleman who resides in Flanders . . . Night is nearing . . . you cannot repair this coach before tomorrow. You are out luck . . . please accept the hospitality of my chateau . . . Mercy! If he discovers our true identities, we'll be lost! I accept, Sire! Who could these women be? See more in No. 7.
