Abstract: 
Nène, No. 7 (continuation). Fantoune, the laundress, was a big gossip, and having noticed Madeleine, ran over to tell her about the rumors that were already circulating. - Michel Corbier is absent, isn't he with Violette in Chantepie? - Are you talking about the seamstress? The master doesn't know her. - Looks like he got to know her. - It's all lies and it is bad of you to repeat it. - Talk to Boisseriot, he'll tell you when the marriage is. Next to Fantoune, Nène contained her pain; but once the laundress had gone, she let it out. The secret dream that grew in her breast like a white bush under tall trees was sacked, brutally taken away. After reflection Madeleine was reassured: the little seamstress would not want to take her place; there was too much work that day. - Other hungry little beaks watch her too …. Nène, more! Nène! … The name resonated all day from twittering lips, embracing her like a fragile bird call, and Nène felt rewarded for her pains. Quickly! … She hardly had time to do laundry before it was time for supper. The air was quick and young; the Earth was steaming, and Michel felt the joy of the moment. But her thought, instead of going to the animals and her work; she went to Violette, who rejoined her. - The pond was surrounded by a mist, it is time that she be within my view. - Good evening, did you hear me come? - I thought of you all day, and wherever you were, I heard your steps once you started approaching me. - My heart reaches a hundred times farther than my ears. - Ah! Violette, if you'd like me to …. - If you knew how much I loved you, if you love me too, why don't you want to marry me? - Why wait and let our youth flow away? She said neither yes nor no, and he, not knowing what to do, followed her with his eyes, as if falling into reverie of adventure. Watch more in no. 8.
