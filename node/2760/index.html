Abstract: 
Surcouf (3rd and Final Chapter), The Letter to Bonaparte, No. 1. Surcouf and his companions were taken by the English and now wait for their deaths in a London prison. - SURCOUF! "To General Bonaparte, the first Consul. The rights of humanity require the ending of the privateer war. By ending it, the French government will allow themselves to consider the beginnings of a future peace. William Pitt." Do you value your life, and the life of your companions. Their existence and yours are between your hands. It's a matter of carrying a message to the First Consul. If he accepts, you and your companions would be set free, if not, you will return as a prisoner. Today is August 10th, you must be back by the 10th of September. - Give the commander Surcouf a pass that allows him to travel to France. See more on Number 2.
